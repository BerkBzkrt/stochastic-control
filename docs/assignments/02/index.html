Assignment 2

  1. AM-GM inequality. Consider the problem of maximizing \[ V_t(a) = \max_{(u_1, \dots, u_t) \in \ALPHABET S_t(a)} u_1 ⋅ u_2 \cdots u_t \] where the region \(\ALPHABET S_t(a)\) is determined by the conditions \[ \sum_{i=1}^n u_i = a \quad \text{and} \quad u_i \ge 0. \]

    1. Prove that \(V_t\) satisfies the following recursion \[ V_t(a) = \max_{u \in [0,a]} x ⋅ V_{n- 1}(a - u), \quad n > 1 \] with \(V_1(a) = a\).

    2. Show inductively that \(V_t(a) = a^t/t^t\) and hence establish the arithmetic-geometric mean inequality \[ \left( \frac{x_1 + \cdots + x_t}{t} \right) \ge ( x_1 \cdots x_t)^{1/t} \]

  2. Monotonicity in time. Suppose that the dynamics is time homogeneous and so is the per-step cost \(c_t(\cdot)\) (except, possibly at the terminal time \(t=T\)). Suppose we have that \(V_{T-1}(x) \le V_T(x)\) for all \(x \in \ALPHABET S\). Then, show that \[ V_{t}(x) \le V_{t+1}(x), \quad \text{for all $x \in \ALPHABET X$ and $t$}.\]

    Similarly, if we have that \(V_{T-1}(x) \ge V_T(x)\) for all \(x \in \ALPHABET X\), then \[ V_{t}(x) \ge V_{t+1}(x), \quad \text{for all $x \in \ALPHABET X$ and $t$}.\]

  3. Dynamic programming for maximizing tail probabilities. Consider a dynamical system that evolves as follows: \[ X_{t+1} = f_t(X_t, U_t, W_t) \] where \(\{X_1, W_1, \dots, W_T\}\) are independent random variables and the control actions \(U_t\) are chosen according to a history dependent policy \(g = (g_1, \dots, g_T)\): \[ U_t = g_t(X_{1:t}, U_{1:t-1}). \] Given a sequence of functions \(h_t \colon \ALPHABET X \mapsto \reals\), the cost of a policy \(g\) is given by the probability that \(h_t(X_t)\) exceeds a given threshold \(α \in \reals\) at some time, i.e., \[ J(g) = \PR^{g}\left( \max_{0 \le t \le T} h_t(X_t) \ge α \right). \]

    Show that the above cost can be put in an additive form that would enable us to use the theory developed in the class to tackle this setup.
