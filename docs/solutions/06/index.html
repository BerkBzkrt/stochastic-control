Problem 1

We start by the right side of the inequality. We want to show that \[V_{k+1} + \bar{\delta}_{k+1} \leq V_k + \bar{\delta}_k.\] We know from the value iteration algorithm proof that \[\mathcal{B} V_k - V_k \leq (1 - \beta) \bar{\delta}_k\] Rewriting this equation, we get \[\begin{equation} V_k + \bar{\delta}_k \geq \mathcal{B} V_k + \beta \bar{\delta}_k \implies V_k + \bar{\delta}_k \geq V_{k+1} + \beta \bar{\delta}_k. \label{eq:one} \end{equation}\]

By definition, we have \[\begin{align} \bar{\delta}_{k+1} &= \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \max_x \{ V_{k+1}(x) - V_k(x) \} = \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \| V_{k+1} - V_k \| \notag \\ &= \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \| \mathcal{B}V_k - \mathcal{B}V_{k-1} \| \notag \\ &\leq \frac{\beta^2}{1-\beta} \| V_{k} - V_{k-1} \| = \beta \bar{\delta}_k. \label{eq:two} \end{align} \]

From \eqref{eq:one} and \eqref{eq:two}, we have that \[V_{k+1} + \bar{\delta}_{k+1} \leq V_{k+1} + \beta \bar{\delta}_k \leq V_k + \bar{\delta}_k.\]

This proves one side of the inequality. For the other side, we have to show that \[V_{k+1} + \underline{\delta}_{k+1} \geq V_k + \underline{\delta}_k.\] We know from the value iteration algorithm proof that \[\mathcal{B} V_k - V_k \geq (1 - \beta) \underline{\delta}_k\] Rewriting this equation, we get \[\begin{equation} V_k + \underline{\delta}_k \leq \mathcal{B} V_k + \beta \underline{\delta}_k \implies V_k + \underline{\delta}_k \leq V_{k+1} + \beta \underline{\delta}_k. \label{eq:three} \end{equation}\]

By definition, we have \[\begin{align} \underline{\delta}_{k+1} &= \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \min_{x} \{ V_{k+1}(x) - V_k(x) \} \notag \\ &= \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \min_{x} \{ \mathcal B V_{k}(x) - \mathcal B V_k(x) \} \notag \\ &\geq \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \min_{x} \{ \mathcal B_{g_k} V_{k}(x) - \mathcal B_{g_k} V_k(x) \} \notag \\ &\geq \frac{\beta^2}{1-\beta} \| V_{k} - V_{k-1} \| = \beta \underline{\delta}_k. \label{eq:four} \end{align} \]

From \eqref{eq:three} and \eqref{eq:four}, we have that \[V_{k+1} + \underline{\delta}_{k+1} \geq V_{k+1} + \beta \underline{\delta}_k \geq V_k + \underline1{\delta}_k.\]

This proves the other side of the inequality.

Problem 3

Proof of Prop. 6:

The proof of the first part is similar to the proof of Prop. 5. We know that \[ V + m \geq \mathcal{B} = \mathcal{B}_g V.\] By the monotonicity and discounting properties, we get \[\mathcal{B}_gV + \beta m \geq \mathcal{B}_g^2 V.\] Again, from the discounting and monotonicity properties, we get \[\mathcal{B}_g^2V + \beta^2 m \geq \mathcal{B}_g^3 V.\] Continuing this way, we get \[\begin{equation} \label{eq:k} \mathcal{B}_g^{k-1}V + \beta^{k-1} m \geq \mathcal{B}_g^k V. \end{equation}\]

Adding all of the above equations, we get \[ \mathcal B_g V + \sum_{\ell = 1}^{k-1} β^{\ell } m \ge \mathcal B^{k} V. \] The result then follows from observing that \(\mathcal B_g V = \mathcal B V\) and \[ \sum_{\ell = 1}^{k-1} \beta^\ell m \le \sum_{\ell = 1}^\infty \beta^\ell m = \frac{\beta}{1 - \beta} m. \] This completes the proof of the first part.

For the second part, observe that \[\mathcal B(\mathcal B_g^{\ell} V) \le \mathcal B_g(\mathcal B_g^{\ell} V) = \mathcal B_g^{\ell + 1} V.\] The result follows from \eqref{eq:k}.

Proof of Prop. 7

We prove the result by induction.

By assumption, we have that \(V_0 + m \ge \mathcal B V_0 = \mathcal B_{g_0} V_0\). Then, by the first property of Prop. 6, we have \[\mathcal B_{g_0}^{\ell_0} V_0 \le \mathcal B V_0 + \frac {\beta}{1 - \beta}m\] and by the second property of Prop. 6, we have \[\mathcal B_{g_0}^{\ell_0} V_0 + \beta^{\ell_0} m \ge \mathcal B(\mathcal B_{g_0}^{\ell_0} V_0). \] This establishes the first property of Prop. 7 for \(k=0\) and forms the basis of induction.

Now assume that the first property is true for \(k\). We want to show that it is true for \(k+1\).

From the left hand side of the induction hypothesis we have, \[\mathcal{B} V_k - \alpha_k m \leq V_k.\] which means \[\mathcal{B} V_k \leq V_k + \alpha_k m.\] Now from Prop. 6, with \(V = V_k\), \(g = g_k\), and \(\ell=\ell_k\), we have \[\mathcal{B} V_k +\frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \alpha_k m \geq \mathcal{B}_{g_k} V_k = V_{k+1}\] which shows the right hand side of the induction for \(k+1\).

Also from Prop. 6, with \(V = V_k\), \(g = g_k\), and \(\ell=\ell_k\), we have \[\begin{align*} V_{k+1} = \mathcal{B}_{g_k} V_k &\geq \mathcal{B} (\mathcal{B}_{g_k} V_k) - \beta^{m_k} \alpha_k m \notag \\ &= \mathcal{B} V_{k+1} - \beta^{m_k} \alpha_k m \notag \\ &= \mathcal{B} V_{k+1} - \alpha_{k+1} m. \end{align*}\] This estabishes the left hand side for \(k+1\). Thus, the first property holds by induction.

We now show the second property of Prop. 7. From the first property, we know \[V_k \geq \mathcal{B} V_k - \alpha_k m\] Using this and Prop. 5 for \(k=0\) we have \[V_k + \frac{m\alpha_k}{1-\beta} \geq V^*\] Now we know that \(\alpha_k \leq \beta^k\). Hence \[V_k + \frac{m\beta^k}{1-\beta} \geq V_k + \frac{m\alpha_k}{1-\beta} \geq V^*\] which shows the right hand side of the second part of Prop 7.

For the left hand side we use the results of the first part of Prop 7. We know that \[\mathcal{B} V_j \geq V_{j+1} - \frac{\beta}{1-\beta} \alpha_j m\] We apply \(\mathcal{B}^{k-j-1}\) to both sides and use monotonicity and contraction properties. Hence we have, \[\mathcal{B}^{k-j} V_j \geq \mathcal{B}^{k-j-1} V_{j+1} - \frac{\beta^{k-j}}{1-\beta} \alpha_j m\] By using the fact that \(\alpha_j \leq \beta^j\) and adding the above equation from \(j=0\) to \(j=k-1\) we have \[\mathcal{B}^{k} V_0 \geq V_{k} - \sum_{j=0}^{k-1} \frac{\beta^{k-j}}{1-\beta} \beta^j m = V_{k} - \frac{k \beta^{k}}{1-\beta} m\]

Now, by an arguement similar to Prop. 5, we can show that if \[V + m \leq \mathcal{B} V \implies $V^* \ge \mathcal B^k V + \frac{\beta^k}{1-\beta}m.\]

Using this result for \(V_0\), by assumption we have \(V_0 + m \le \mathcal B V_0\). Thus, \[V^* \geq \mathcal{B}^k V_0 - \frac{\beta^k}{1-\beta}m, \quad k=0,1,\dots\] Now combining this inequality with the one above we can show the left hand side of the last equation in prop 7 and the proof is complete.
